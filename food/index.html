Food (recipes and reminders)

Listed below are recipes and (short) briefs on things I like to make. They’re here for quick and easy access — because different friends kept asking me for instructions on how to make “the red wine pasta” and I had to tell them to “Google the New York Times recipe” alongside an accompanying voice note that lasted a little longer than three minutes. Saving these recipes online in my little corner of the internets, honestly, just makes more sense.

For the more fleshed out recipes, I did want to cut out the extraneous information and imagery in lieu of a very clean reading experience and faster loading. I cut out the photos — the extra pressure on plating and trying to make Beautiful, Natural Light-Filled Images of the Process is something I do not want right now. But, in the end, it turns out I do enjoy explaining things. So, yes, sorry, this is also one of Those food sections. I lack the ads and auto-playing videos though.

I grew up in a family and community of cooks and chefs: “Did you eat today?” is how we say I love you. ("What did you eat today?" is my mother checking to make sure I have not counted cereal as a legitimate lunch meal.) I spent my childhood in-and-out of kitchens — both domestic and industrial. I love the process of cooking: the different cuts, the stirs, when you know by look, smell, and taste whether something is ready or not. And so, I am not great at measurements — but I have tried to add them in the posts listed below. If they are woefully unhelpful, I apologise. At least you are not asking my mother whose only response would be: “Add however much you would like.”

And lastly, at the time of writing (the glorious year of 2020), we have an interesting oven that likes to surprise us with temperatures so when an oven is involved, please note that those times are estimates.
