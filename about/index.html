About

Last updated: 2019

Amsterdam-based. Speaks in an English accent. Occasional podcast guest.

I read History with a focus on humanitarianism; and have been working in the Netherlands since 2015 in the spaces of community, sustainability, and sports.

I like pastel de queijo, taking photographs, and terribly bad movies — sometimes all at once.

.

Instead of keeping a private journal, I thought it would be interesting – a little throwback, if you like – to publish this online, for friends and strangers. It is like Christopher Biscardi’s digital garden:

A Digital Garden is lowercase b blogging. It’s writing without worrying about what people will think. It’s writing in small, unfinished pieces and building them up to larger more edited pieces, in public. This is in contrast to big B blogging, which can be thought of as writing a 6k+ word thinkpiece or the authoritative work on topic X without shipping any smaller pieces.

I also really liked Joel Hooks' more longer explanation.

So, yes! Welcome to my digital garden.
